      Combat Rules

      Custom D&D formulas for Pokémon combat

      Hit Points

      HP = base_hp + (level × 2)

      Every Pokémon's max HP scales linearly with level. A level 5 Rattata (base HP 30) has 40 HP.

      Armor Class

      maxDef = ⌊⌊2 × base_def + 99⌋ × 1.1⌋ (perfect EVs/IVs/+nature) AC = round( (maxDef + level − 1) / 20 )

      AC is derived from a Pokémon's base Defense stat, scaled to represent perfect defensive investment. AC grows slightly with level to reflect experience.

      To-Hit Bonus

      divisor = 10 (levels 1–25) = 9 (levels 26–50) = 8 (levels 51–75) = 7 (levels 76–99) hit bonus = round(move_accuracy / divisor)

      Each move's accuracy becomes a flat +N to hit bonus. The divisor decreases at higher levels so attacks land more reliably as Pokémon grow stronger.

      Moves with no accuracy (e.g. Swift) always hit automatically.

      Attack Roll

      roll = d20 + hit_bonus vs opponent AC
      • Roll equal to or above the opponent's AC → HIT
      • Roll below the opponent's AC → MISS

      Click any move in a fighter's move list during combat to instantly roll and see the result.

      Damage

      damage = 1d8 (default)

      On a hit, roll the damage die shown next to the move. The default damage die is 1d8 for all moves. The DM may adjust this based on move power or situation.

      Turn Order & Rounds

      Initiative

      At the start of combat each player rolls a d20 (or uses Roll All) to determine their initiative. Higher initiative goes first within matchups.

      Speed

      Within each matchup, the Pokémon with the higher Speed stat attacks first. Ties go to the party Pokémon.

      Rounds

      Each round all matchups resolve. Click Next Round to advance. Rounds are tracked in the header — use them to pace spells, status effects, or other time-based mechanics.
